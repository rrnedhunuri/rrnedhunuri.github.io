A Localized Eco-Hydrologic Model, Current and Future Habitat Restoration Scenarios for the entire DeLuca Property

The DeLuca Preserve is a crucial nature corridor between the Kissimmee Prairie Preserve State Park and the Three Lakes Wildlife Management Area, reducing habitat fragmentation and supporting endangered species. It is vital for conserving the Everglades Headwaters and contributes to the Kissimmee River and St. Johns River watersheds

However, human activities have altered its hydrology. This project involves developing a high-resolution eco-hydrologic model to support habitat restoration and management strategies, considering water quality, soil-water-microbiome interactions, biodiversity, and ecosystem functions.
